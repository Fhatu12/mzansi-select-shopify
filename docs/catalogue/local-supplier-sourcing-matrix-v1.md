## Purpose

This document records the approved local-first sourcing strategy for the MVP launch catalogue, the supplier-prioritisation tiers, the launch collection supplier matrix, the working product status model, the Slice 13C preview shortlist, the Slice 13D supplier proof-pack status, the Slice 13E supplier proof gap-closure status, the Slice 13F preview-staging preparation-pack status, and the Slice 13I controlled Admin preview-only listing outcome for five approved handles.

## Approved strategy

- Shift launch catalogue sourcing to a local-first South African supplier model.
- Reason: reduce fulfilment risk, long delivery delays, refund risk, customer trust damage, and order-fulfilment uncertainty from international suppliers.
- International and older candidate supplier paths are paused unless explicitly reopened.

## Primary recommended suppliers

### 1. ZA Dropshipping

- Best Shopify integration and local-stock test candidate.
- Use only as a small pilot first because public Shopify app review confidence is weak.

### 2. Neat Freak

- Best category fit for `Home & Living`, `Kitchen & Storage`, `Office & Desk`, storage, organisers, kitchen, laundry, and household utility.
- Must confirm wholesale / dropship terms and image-usage rights.

### 3. Gadgetgyz

- Best `Tech Accessories` test supplier.
- Must confirm stock, pricing, fulfilment workflow, delivery SLA, and return terms.

## Conditional suppliers

- `Men’s Republic`: verify supplier identity and current dropship / trade access before MVP reliance.
- `Third Wave Distribution`: verify direct because public signals are not clean enough for launch reliance.

## Deferred suppliers

- `Dropstore`: defer until the exact entity and integration path are verified.
- `Mantality`: use as benchmark or manual supplier only if trade / dropship terms exist.
- `The Scents`: defer because fragrance / beauty is outside current launch taxonomy and carries claim / return risk.
- `Wheeltrend`: defer to later print-on-demand or branded merch.
- `Printify` / `Printful`: defer to later branded merch because international delivery and fulfilment timing do not solve the current local-fulfilment risk.

## Commercial readiness note

- Current margin calculations are product-only estimates based on public supplier prices.
- They assume shipping is charged separately to the customer or otherwise recovered.
- Product-only margins are promising, but final margins remain blocked until the shipping-cost handling model is confirmed.
- If shipping is customer-paid or otherwise recovered, the shortlist remains viable.
- If shipping is absorbed by Mzansi Select, low-ticket items likely need bundles or minimum-cart rules.
- No final pricing is approved.

## Fulfilment risk

- `Neat Freak` is promising for local sourcing, but order timing must not be overpromised.
- Public shipping guidance indicates orders can take `7-14` working days, with most sent within `2-7` working days after payment confirmation, plus courier delivery after dispatch.
- `Gadgetgyz` has useful product and dropship signals, but exact shipping fee, SLA, and fulfilment workflow still need direct confirmation.
- Delivery wording must remain cautious, and product copy should stay factual and conservative until stronger supplier-backed evidence exists.
- Do not approve best-seller, premium-quality, guaranteed-stock, delivery-promise, or supplier-backed claims unless evidence confirms them.

## Global blockers

- Wholesale / dropship terms for `Neat Freak`
- Direct fulfilment workflow
- Shipping-cost handling model
- Image usage permission
- Stock reliability proof
- Sample / product quality proof
- Final target selling range confidence
- Return / refund practicality under the Mzansi Select support model
- Final Product Owner commercial decision
